The Faculty Guide Role

The Faculty Guide is the bridge between academic research and the digital repository. Their role is deeply rooted in mentorship and quality control. Unlike the Admin, the Guide works directly with student teams, supervising the lifecycle of a project from its initial registration to the final document upload.

A Guide is responsible for ensuring that every project uploaded to SPARK meets the department's academic standards. They manage the specific metadata of each project, including team member details and batch information. By serving as the primary uploader, the Guide ensures that only verified, high-quality research is entered into the college's permanent digital archives.

Key Operations:

  • Direct supervision of undergraduate and postgraduate research teams.
  • Accurate registration of student groups and project titles.
  • Uploading and managing research files (PDF/ZIP) for departmental records.
